Frequently Asked Questions

What is domestic violence?

Domestic violence is any pattern of behavior used to gain power and control over a partner or family member. It can include physical, emotional, and psychological abuse.

How can I get help?

If you are in immediate danger, call 911. For non-emergency situations, reach out to local shelters or counseling services listed in our resources section.

What are my legal rights as a victim?

Victims of domestic violence have the right to file charges, seek protection orders, and access legal aid. More information is available on our Legal Guidance page.

How can I support someone experiencing domestic violence?

You can support them by listening without judgment, helping them find resources, and encouraging them to reach out for professional help.

What should I do if I witness domestic violence?

If you witness domestic violence, it is crucial to ensure your safety first. If it is safe, intervene and call the authorities. You can also provide support to the victim afterward.

Are there shelters available for victims of domestic violence?

Yes, there are many shelters and safe houses available. You can find information on local shelters in our resources section or contact a hotline for assistance.

Can domestic violence happen to anyone?

Yes, domestic violence can affect anyone regardless of gender, age, race, or socioeconomic status. It is a widespread issue that can happen in any community.

What resources are available for victims?

Victims can access a range of resources including hotlines, legal assistance, counseling, and support groups. Check our resources section for more information.

How can I create a safety plan?

A safety plan includes identifying a safe place to go, having a packed bag ready, keeping important documents accessible, and having a code word to communicate with trusted friends or family.
