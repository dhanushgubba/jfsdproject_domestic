How to Get Help

Learn how to seek help and find emergency contacts.

Immediate Resources for Emergency Situations

If you are in immediate danger, call your local emergency services or a trusted friend or family member. Here are some additional resources:

  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: Call 1-800-799-SAFE (7233) for confidential support 24/7.
  • Local Shelters: Check for shelters in your area that can provide safe housing and resources.
  • Crisis Centers: Reach out to local crisis centers for immediate support and counseling.

How to Create a Safety Plan

Creating a safety plan can help you prepare for a potential emergency situation. Here are some steps to consider:

  • Identify a safe place to go in case of an emergency.
  • Keep important documents and essentials in a safe location.
  • Have a list of emergency contacts saved on your phone.
  • Plan how to leave your home quickly if needed.

Additional Resources

Here are more resources you can utilize:

  • National Coalition Against Domestic Violence: Visit their website for more information and resources.
  • RAINN (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network): Call 1-800-656-HOPE (4673) for support.
  • Local Hotlines: Search for local hotlines for immediate assistance.
